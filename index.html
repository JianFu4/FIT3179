COMPARE BETWEEN CATEGORY

Over the span of 20 years, the electricity generation landscape has exhibited intriguing trends. Notably, the proportion of electricity derived from fossil fuels has remained relatively stable. At the same time, renewable energy sources have progressively increased their share, gradually encroaching upon the portion previously dominated by nuclear energy.

A comparative assessment across five distinct regions provides further depth into the energy dynamics.A notable observation is the gradual decline in fossil fuels usage within the Americas year by year. In contrast, renewable energy, particularly in Asia, showcases a promising growth trend, reinforcing the global shift towards sustainable energy sources.




OVERVIEW AND DETAILS

We aim to explore the correlation between CO2 emmisions and electricity produced by fossil fuels. It is evident from the data that these two variables are intrinsically linked. A rise in electricity generation from fossil fuels invariably leads to increased CO2 emissions. A noteworthy observation is China's escalating trend in both electricity production from fossil fuels and CO2 emissions. Remarkably, within a span of two decades, China surpassed the United States in this regard.
